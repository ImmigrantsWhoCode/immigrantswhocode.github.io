Our Mission

The mission of Immigrants Who Code is to provide resources to immigrants in order to facilitate their continued education while dealing with their various immigration issues. The world of coding is raidly expnading, and it is crucial that immigrants not be left out. There are many Bootcamps and other educational resources that cater to women and other minorities, except non U.S citizens and non-permanent residents. This website is advocating for the inclusion of minorities in the learning process,regardless of their level of citizenship.

Specifically this website will seek to cater to:

  • Refugees:
    • a person who has been forced to leave their country in order to escape war, persecution, or natural disaster.
  • Asylum Seekers
    • a person who has left their home country as a political refugee and is seeking asylum in another
  • Asylees
    • a person who is seeking or has been granted political asylum
  • Other groups of similarly displaced immigrants
    • *This list is not exhaustive as there are many different kinds of immigrants, all with different restrictions in terms of education and job opportunities.

Resources

Listed below are some great free resources to help you get started on your coding journey

    CodeAcademy

    Codecademy has both a free and paid version. The free version is very comprehensive, and is more than enough to get you started

    W3Schools

    W3Schools is completely free and is a great reference to check a topic really quickly.

    Mozilla Developer Network

    The Mozilla Developer Network is where you go to get the most comprehensive and up to information with regards to HTML, CSS and JavaScript.

    Free Code Camp

    Free Code Camp is an open source platform designed to take you from a complete novice to a web developer in your own time.

    GA Dash

    The GA Dash Assembly is a free program by the folks at General Assembly.

    Khan Academy

    KhanAcademy is used by many to learn a wide variety of subjects, not just web development.

    The Odin Project

    The Odin project is an open source web development curriculum designed to take you from beginner to hired.

    This list is by no means exhaustive, but this is a good place to start learning coding quickly, and get projects up and running quickly. This list will be updated periodically to include useful Youtube channels, blogs and articles that will be helpful to new developers.
